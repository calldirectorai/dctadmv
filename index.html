The curriculum

Six weeks. Six modules. One job-ready technician.

01

Data Center Fundamentals & Safety

How a modern facility is built and why uptime is everything.

  • Tiers, redundancy (N+1, 2N) and the meaning of "five nines"
  • Floor safety, ESD, lockout/tagout, and PPE
  • Reading a facility map and asset tags
02

Server Hardware & Rack/Stack

The core skill: getting hardware mounted, connected, and online.

  • Rack units, rails, cable arms, and weight loading
  • Installing servers, PDUs, switches, and blanking panels
  • Component swaps: drives, RAM, PSUs, and field diagnostics
03

Structured Cabling & Networking Basics

Clean cabling is a craft — and a hiring differentiator.

  • Copper & fiber termination, testing, and labeling standards
  • Patch panels, cross-connects, and cable management
  • IP basics, VLANs, and what a NOC actually watches
04

Power, UPS & Cooling

The systems that keep the floor alive 24/7.

  • Power distribution, UPS, generators, and dual-feed
  • Hot/cold aisle containment and airflow management
  • Reading environmental sensors and responding to alarms
05

Monitoring, DCIM & Ticketing

How work actually flows on a real shift.

  • DCIM tools, dashboards, and capacity tracking
  • Working tickets, smart-hands requests, and escalation
  • Documentation that keeps you and the next tech safe
06

Career Readiness & Capstone

Turn your new skills into an offer letter.

  • Resume, LinkedIn, and interview practice for DC roles
  • Hands-on capstone: build, cable, and document a rack
  • Introductions to local hiring partners
